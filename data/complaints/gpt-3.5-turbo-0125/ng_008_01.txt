COMPLAINT FOR NEGLIGENCE

COMES NOW, Plaintiff Emily Carter, by and through undersigned counsel, and hereby files this Complaint against Quincy Dining LLC, and alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff, Emily Carter, is an individual residing at [Your Address], [City], Massachusetts.

2. Defendant, Quincy Dining LLC, is a business entity registered in Massachusetts, located at 123 Main St, Quincy, MA 02169.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 218, Section 19.

4. Venue is proper in this Court as the incident giving rise to this action occurred at 123 Main St, Quincy, MA 02169, which falls within the jurisdiction of the Quincy District Court.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On August 15, 2023, at approximately [time], at the premises located at 123 Main St, Quincy, MA 02169 owned and operated by the Defendant, Quincy Dining LLC, Plaintiff Emily Carter was a customer dining at the establishment.

6. At said premises, a server employed by the Defendant negligently dropped a tray of water glasses in the narrow hallway between the kitchen and the dining room, causing a spillage of water and ice on the tile floor.

7. Despite acknowledging the spill and expressing intention to clean it up, the server failed to promptly remedy the hazardous condition, leaving the wet floor without warning signs or barriers.

8. Subsequently, Plaintiff, unaware of the hazard, proceeded to use the bathroom and slipped on the wet surface, resulting in a severe knee injury that required medical treatment and rehabilitation.

9. It was revealed that other employees had been aware of the hazard caused by the spill, indicating a pattern of negligence and disregard for customer safety on the part of the Defendant.

CAUSES OF ACTION

10. Plaintiff realleges and incorporates by reference the preceding paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

11. The Defendant, Quincy Dining LLC, owed a duty of care to Plaintiff Emily Carter to maintain its premises in a safe condition and to promptly remedy known hazards to prevent injuries to customers.

12. The Defendant breached its duty of care by failing to timely clean up the hazardous spill, failing to provide warning signs, and failing to ensure the safety of its patrons.

13. As a direct and proximate result of the Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff suffered a serious knee injury, incurring medical expenses of approximately $2,200 and lost wages totaling $1,500.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Emily Carter respectfully requests that this Honorable Court enter judgment in her favor and against the Defendant Quincy Dining LLC, as follows:

a. Compensatory damages in the amount of $3,700;
b. Prejudgment and post-judgment interest as allowed by law;
c. Costs of suit; and
d. Any other relief that the Court deems just and proper.
