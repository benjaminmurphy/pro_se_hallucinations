COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, Plaintiff Alex Rivera, by and through counsel, and files this Complaint against Defendant Jordan Smith and alleges as follows:

1. PARTIES

1.1 Plaintiff Alex Rivera is an individual residing at [Your Address], [City, State, Zip].

1.2 Defendant Jordan Smith is an individual upon whom process may be served at 1234 Commonwealth Avenue, Boston, MA 02215.

2. JURISDICTION AND VENUE

2.1 This action arises under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

2.2 This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as the events giving rise to this action occurred within Suffolk County.

3. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

3.1 On September 15, 2023, at 1234 Commonwealth Avenue, Boston, MA 02215, Plaintiff was lawfully riding a bicycle in a marked bike lane in accordance with traffic rules.

3.2 Defendant, a rideshare driver, negligently opened his car door into the marked bike lane without checking for oncoming bicyclists, causing Plaintiff to crash into the door and suffer injuries.

3.3 Plaintiff sustained road rash on arms and legs, a chipped front tooth, and damage to the bicycle frame as a direct result of the Defendant's negligence.

3.4 Defendant admitted at the scene that he did not see Plaintiff due to checking his phone, subsequently providing false information to his insurance company to deny liability.

4. CAUSES OF ACTION

4.1 Plaintiff alleges that Defendant's actions constitute negligence under Massachusetts law by failing to exercise reasonable care in opening the car door in a manner that endangered passing bicyclists. (See Garrity v. Daly, 389 Mass. 21 (1983); Green v. Boston and Worcester St. Ry. Co., 201 Mass. 241 (1909)).

4.2 Defendant's negligence directly caused Plaintiff's injuries and resulting damages, including medical expenses and lost wages.

5. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in their favor against Defendant, and award the following relief:

5.1 Compensatory damages in the amount of $2,500 for medical expenses and $800 for lost wages;

5.2 Punitive damages as deemed just and proper by the Court;

5.3 Prejudgment and postjudgment interest as allowed by law;

5.4 Court costs and attorney fees; and

5.5 Such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.

6. JURY DEMAND

Plaintiff requests a trial by jury on all triable issues.
