COMPLAINT

Now comes the Plaintiff, Maria Rodriguez, by and through undersigned counsel, and for her Complaint against the Defendant, John Smith, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff, Maria Rodriguez, is an individual residing at 45 Washington Ave, Chelsea, MA 02150.

2. Defendant, John Smith, is the landlord of the aforementioned property.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This action arises under Massachusetts law and jurisdiction of this Court is proper as the events giving rise to this action occurred in Chelsea, MA.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

4. On January 15, 2022, Plaintiff entered into a residential lease agreement with Defendant for the property located at 45 Washington Ave, Chelsea, MA 02150.

5. The main entry door to the building in which Plaintiff's rented apartment is located has a broken lock that does not properly latch, despite Plaintiff's repeated complaints to Defendant about this security concern. Defendant promised to fix the lock but has failed to do so even after multiple reminders from Plaintiff.

6. As a direct result of the broken lock, unknown individuals gained unauthorized access to the building, leading to theft of packages belonging to Plaintiff and other residents. Plaintiff suffered a direct loss of approximately $120 due to stolen packages.

7. Plaintiff also discovered evidence suggesting that an unauthorized individual has been sleeping in the basement of the building, causing Plaintiff significant distress and concern for her safety and security.

8. In response to the security issues caused by Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff purchased a security camera and a better deadbolt at a cost of approximately $200 to safeguard her rented premises.

CAUSE OF ACTION

9. The Defendant's failure to maintain a secure and habitable living environment constitutes a breach of the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment owed to the Plaintiff under Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 186, ยง 14.
