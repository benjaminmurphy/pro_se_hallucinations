COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, Plaintiff Emily Johnson, by and through undersigned counsel, and files this Complaint against Defendant Mark Thompson, and alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff Emily Johnson is an individual residing at 25 Cedar Street, Brockton, MA 02302.

2. Defendant Mark Thompson is the landlord of the aforementioned property.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 218, Section 21. Venue is proper in the Brockton District Court as the subject property is located within its jurisdiction.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

4. Plaintiff entered into a lease agreement with Defendant on January 15, 2022, to rent an apartment located at 25 Cedar Street, Brockton, MA 02302, for a monthly rent of $1500.

5. On [Date Hot Water Went Out], the hot water in the apartment ceased to function. Plaintiff promptly notified Defendant of the issue.

6. Defendant informed Plaintiff that the water heater required a part which was on backorder.

7. Despite repeated assurances from Defendant that the issue would be promptly resolved, Plaintiff has been without hot water for almost a month, causing significant inconvenience and hardship.

8. As a result of the lack of hot water, Plaintiff has incurred additional expenses amounting to approximately $450, including costs for alternative showering arrangements and gas expenses.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

9. Plaintiff alleges that Defendant's failure to provide hot water for an extended period of time constitutes a breach of the implied warranty of habitability and the covenant of quiet enjoyment under Massachusetts law.
