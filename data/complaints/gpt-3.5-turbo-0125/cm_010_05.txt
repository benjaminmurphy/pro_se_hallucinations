6. Since the issuance of the original custody order, significant changes in circumstances have occurred, warranting a modification of the custody arrangement. Plaintiff has completed her education, obtained stable employment with normal hours, and now resides in a two-bedroom apartment suitable for the child, providing her a dedicated room.

8. The Defendant has been uncooperative and resistant to any discussion regarding modifying the existing custody arrangement, displaying anger when the child expresses her wishes.

IV. Legal Basis

9. Modification of a custody order requires a showing of a substantial and material change in circumstances since the entry of the prior judgment, as outlined in Yannas v. Frondistou-Yannas, 395 Mass. 704 (1985).

10. Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 208, Section 28, establishes the statutory authority of the Court to modify custody orders based on a significant change in circumstances affecting the child's best interests.

V. Requested Relief

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Honorable Court modify the existing custody order to grant increased parenting time to the Plaintiff, Jessica Harrington, consistent with the child's expressed desire for more overnights and in consideration of the substantial and material changes in circumstances since the entry of the original order.
