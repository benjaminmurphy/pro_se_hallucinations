COMPLAINT

Now comes the Plaintiff, Emily Carter, and for her Complaint against the Defendant, John Thompson, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. The Plaintiff, Emily Carter, is an individual residing at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, MA 01103.

2. The Defendant, John Thompson, is the landlord of the aforementioned property.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 218, Section 4.

4. Venue is proper in the Springfield District Court as the property in question is located within its jurisdiction.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On or about August 1, 2022, the Plaintiff entered into a residential lease agreement with the Defendant for the rental of a two-bedroom apartment located at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, MA 01103.

6. Since on or about October 20, 2025, one of the bedrooms in the apartment has been without electricity due to wiring issues.

7. Despite being informed of the electrical problem, the Defendant has failed to remedy the situation promptly.

8. The Plaintiff has been unable to use the affected room as it is unusable due to the lack of electricity.

9. The Plaintiff has incurred additional expenses, including daily expenditures at coffee shops for workspace and a monthly coworking space membership fee, totaling approximately $150 per month, due to the uninhabitable condition of the apartment space.

CAUSE OF ACTION

10. The Defendant's failure to promptly address and rectify the electrical issue in the Plaintiff's rental apartment constitutes a breach of the implied warranty of habitability and the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff requests that this Court enter judgment in her favor against the Defendant as follows:

1. Award damages in the amount of $900 for the additional expenses and inconvenience incurred.
   
2. Grant any further relief that the Court deems just and proper.
