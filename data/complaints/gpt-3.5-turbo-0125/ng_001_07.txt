COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, Plaintiff Emily Johnson ("Plaintiff"), by and through undersigned counsel, and for her Complaint against Defendant Fresh Mart Grocery ("Defendant"), states as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff Emily Johnson is a resident of Somerville, Middlesex County, Massachusetts.

2. Defendant Fresh Mart Grocery is a business entity operating within the state of Massachusetts and located at 123 Elm Street, Somerville, MA.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 218, Section 3.

4. Venue is proper in Middlesex County Superior Court, Somerville Division, as the events giving rise to this action occurred at the aforementioned Fresh Mart Grocery location.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On August 12, 2023, at the Fresh Mart Grocery located at 123 Elm Street, Somerville, MA, Plaintiff was a patron shopping in the dairy aisle.

6. Plaintiff slipped and fell on a puddle of spilled milk in the aisle, which was not marked with any warning signs.

7. The puddle of spilled milk was of a sizeable nature, evidenced by cart tracks present within it.

8. Despite an employee of Fresh Mart Grocery being aware of the spill for several minutes prior to Plaintiff's fall, no action was taken to clean up the spill or warn customers of the dangerous condition.

9. Following the fall, Plaintiff experienced a hip sprain, necessitating medical treatment including physical therapy.

10. As a result of the injury sustained at the premises of Fresh Mart Grocery, Plaintiff incurred approximately $1,200 in medical expenses and lost earnings amounting to approximately $800 from missed work shifts at her job.

CAUSE OF ACTION

11. Defendant owed a duty of care to Plaintiff to maintain the premises in a safe condition for customers.

12. Defendant breached its duty of care by failing to promptly clean up the spilled milk and failing to warn customers of the hazardous condition, resulting in Plaintiff's injuries.

13. Defendant's breach of duty was a proximate cause of Plaintiff's injuries and damages.

COUNT FOR NEGLIGENCE

14. Plaintiff realleges and incorporates by reference all previous paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

15. Defendant, Fresh Mart Grocery, breached its duty of care owed to Plaintiff by failing to maintain a safe environment and failing to promptly address the spilled milk in the dairy aisle.

16. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff suffered injuries, damages, and losses.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Emily Johnson respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in her favor against Defendant Fresh Mart Grocery for:

a. Compensatory damages in an amount not less than $2,000, plus costs and interest;
b. Any further relief that the Court deems just and proper.

JURY DEMAND

Plaintiff demands a trial by jury on all issues so triable.
