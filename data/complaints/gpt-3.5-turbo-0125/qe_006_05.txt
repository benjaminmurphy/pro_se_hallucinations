FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On January 1, 2022, the Plaintiff entered into a lease agreement with the Defendant to rent an apartment at 123 Main St, Brockton, MA 02301, for a monthly rent of $1450.

6. On November 10, 2025, the hot water in the Plaintiff's apartment ceased to function properly.

7. Immediately upon discovery of the lack of hot water, the Plaintiff notified the Defendant of the issue.

8. Despite being notified of the lack of hot water, the Defendant failed to take prompt and appropriate action to remedy the situation.

9. The Defendant informed the Plaintiff that a part for the water heater was required and subsequently claimed that the part was backordered.

10. As of the date of this Complaint, approximately three weeks have passed since the Plaintiff first reported the lack of hot water, and the issue remains unresolved.

11. The Plaintiff has incurred additional expenses amounting to approximately $30 per month for gym shower usage, as well as gas expenses for travel to the gym due to the lack of hot water.

12. The Plaintiff also had to incur expenses to take her baby to her mother's house for baths due to the lack of hot water in the apartment.

CAUSE OF ACTION

13. The Defendant's failure to provide or promptly repair the hot water system in the Plaintiff's apartment constitutes a breach of the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment under Massachusetts law.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Maria Johnson respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in her favor and against Defendant Robert Smith for the following relief:
