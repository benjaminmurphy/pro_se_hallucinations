COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, Plaintiff Jessica L. Thompson, by and through undersigned counsel, and for her Complaint against Defendant Michael R. Harrison, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff Jessica L. Thompson is an individual residing at 123 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301.

2. Defendant Michael R. Harrison is the landlord of the premises located at 123 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter XX, Section XXX.

4. Venue is proper in the District Court for the County of Brockton, as the subject property is located within its jurisdiction.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. Plaintiff entered into a residential lease agreement with Defendant for the rental of an apartment at 123 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301, commencing on April 1, 2022.

6. On or about November 10, 2025, the hot water in Plaintiff's apartment ceased functioning.

7. Plaintiff promptly notified Defendant of the lack of hot water and Defendant acknowledged the issue.

8. Despite Defendant's assurances of promptly fixing the hot water issue, as of the date of filing this Complaint, December 1, 2025, Plaintiff remains without hot water for nearly a month.

9. Plaintiff has been forced to seek alternative arrangements to maintain personal hygiene, incurring additional costs of approximately $30 per month for gym showers, gas expenses, and the inconvenience of taking her child to another location for baths.

10. Plaintiff is thus seeking damages of approximately $450 for the breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment and the inconvenience caused by Defendant's failure to provide hot water for an extended period.

CAUSE OF ACTION

11. Plaintiff alleges a cause of action for breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment, a violation of Massachusetts landlord-tenant law, M.G.L. c. 186, §§ 14 and 15.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully prays for the following relief:

a. Judgement in favor of Plaintiff for damages of approximately $450;
b. Attorneys' fees and costs as provided by law;
c. Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
