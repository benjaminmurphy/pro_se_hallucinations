III. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS  

5. Plaintiff entered into a residential lease agreement with Defendant for the rental of a two-bedroom apartment located at 123 Main St, Dorchester, MA 02124, commencing on January 1, 2022.

6. Plaintiff has been paying rent in the amount of $1800 per month as required by the lease agreement.

7. From the winter of 2022 onwards, Plaintiff experienced repeated issues with the apartment's heating system, which resulted in substandard and inadequate heating during cold weather.

8. Despite making two attempts to repair the heating system in response to Plaintiff's complaints, Defendant failed to provide a consistent and habitable level of heat within the premises.

9. The recurring heating issues forced Plaintiff to purchase and utilize two space heaters to maintain a livable temperature, leading to an increase in monthly electric bills by approximately $150.

10. As a direct consequence of the inadequate heating conditions in the apartment, Plaintiff suffered from bronchitis in January 2025, which was exacerbated by the cold indoor temperatures, requiring medical treatment, causing missed work, and incurring medical expenses.

IV. CAUSE OF ACTION  

11. Plaintiff alleges a single cause of action for breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment against Defendant pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 186, Section 14, which mandates that landlords must provide tenants with a premises fit for human habitation, including adequate heat during the colder months.

V. PRAYER FOR RELIEF  

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court grant the following relief:

1. Award damages in the amount of $1200 to compensate Plaintiff for increased electric bills and medical expenses incurred due to Defendant's failure to provide adequate heating;
   
2. Grant any additional relief that the Court deems just and proper; and
   
3. Award Plaintiff costs, attorney's fees, and any other relief the Court deems appropriate.
