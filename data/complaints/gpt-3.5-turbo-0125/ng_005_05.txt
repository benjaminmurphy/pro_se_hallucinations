COMPLAINT

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

1. This action arises under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. Jurisdiction is proper in this Court pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 218, Section 3.

2. Venue is proper in Hampden County Superior Court as the events giving rise to the claims occurred at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, MA on March 15, 2023.

PARTIES

3. Plaintiff, Carlos Martinez, is an individual residing at [Your Address], Springfield, MA.

4. Defendant, Thomas Reed, is an individual residing at [Defendant's Address], Springfield, MA.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On March 15, 2023, at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, MA, Plaintiff was backing his car into the driveway shared with Defendant when a ladder owned by Defendant fell due to windy conditions.

6. Plaintiff had previously expressed concerns to Defendant regarding the safety of the ladder being leaned against the fence adjacent to the parking area.

7. Despite Plaintiff's warnings, Defendant failed to secure the ladder properly, leading to it falling onto Plaintiff's vehicle, causing damage to the hood and windshield.

8. As a result of the incident, Plaintiff incurred expenses for the repair of the vehicle, including the cost of a new windshield and bodywork.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

9. Defendant owed a duty of care to Plaintiff to maintain his property in a safe condition and failed to take reasonable precautions to prevent the ladder from falling.

10. Defendant breached the duty of care owed to Plaintiff by failing to secure the ladder properly, leading to damage to Plaintiff's vehicle.

11. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff suffered damages in the form of repair costs for the vehicle, inconvenience, and related expenses.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Carlos Martinez respectfully requests that this Honorable Court enter judgment in his favor and against Defendant Thomas Reed for:

a. Compensatory damages in an amount to be proven at trial but not less than $0 for repair costs and related expenses;
b. Prejudgment and postjudgment interest as allowed by law;
c. Costs and expenses of this action; and
d. Such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.
