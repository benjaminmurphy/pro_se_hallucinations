FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

4. On June 1, 2022, Plaintiff entered into a rental agreement with Defendant for the apartment located at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, MA 01103.

5. Since approximately October 20, 2025, Plaintiff has been experiencing a complete lack of electricity in one of the two bedrooms in the rented apartment.

6. Despite notice to the Defendant regarding the lack of electricity, Defendant has failed to remedy the situation promptly.

7. Defendant commissioned an electrician visit the premises and the electrician advised that the necessary repairs were significant due to outdated wiring in the building.

8. As a result, Plaintiff has been unable to use the affected bedroom, which was previously utilized as a home office.

9. In light of the loss of access to the bedroom, Plaintiff has incurred expenses for alternative working arrangements, including daily costs at coffee shops totaling approximately $15 per day, along with a $150 monthly membership at a coworking space.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

10. Breach of the Covenant of Quiet Enjoyment: Defendant has breached the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment pursuant to Massachusetts law by failing to provide habitable premises in accordance with the rental agreement, resulting in significant inconvenience and financial loss to the Plaintiff.
