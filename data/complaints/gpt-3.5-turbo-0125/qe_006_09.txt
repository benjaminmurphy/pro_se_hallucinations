COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, Plaintiff Jessica Torres, by and through undersigned counsel, and files this Complaint against Defendant Mark Thompson, and alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff Jessica Torres is an individual residing at 123 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301.

2. Defendant Mark Thompson is the landlord of the abovementioned premises.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. The District Court for Plymouth County has jurisdiction over this matter under Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 218, Section 21.

4. Venue is proper in Brockton, Massachusetts, as the subject property is located in this jurisdiction.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. Plaintiff entered into a lease agreement with Defendant for the rental of an apartment at 123 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301, commencing on January 1, 2022.

6. On or about November 10, 2025, Plaintiff reported to Defendant that there was no hot water in the apartment. Defendant acknowledged the issue and informed Plaintiff that a part needed to be replaced in the water heater.

7. Despite Defendant's assurances that the part was ordered, subsequent delays occurred. Plaintiff has been without hot water for approximately three weeks since reporting the issue.

8. As a result of the lack of hot water, Plaintiff has incurred financial damages amounting to approximately $450, which include additional expenses for gym showers, gas costs, and bathing facilities for her baby.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

9. Defendant's failure to provide hot water constitutes a breach of the implied warranty of habitability and the covenant of quiet enjoyment under Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 186, Section 14.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests judgment against Defendant as follows:

A. $450 in compensatory damages;
B. Any additional costs deemed appropriate by the Court;
C. Prejudgment interest as allowed by law;
D. Costs and attorney fees as permitted by law; and
E. Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
