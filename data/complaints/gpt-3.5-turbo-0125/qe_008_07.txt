COMPLAINT

Now comes the Plaintiff, Jessica Monroe, and for her Complaint against the Defendant, Harold Wiggins, states as follows:

PARTIES

1. The Plaintiff, Jessica Monroe, is an individual residing at 275 Washington Ave, Chelsea, MA 02150.

2. The Defendant, Harold Wiggins, is the landlord responsible for the premises located at the aforementioned address.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This action arises under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, specifically the Massachusetts landlord-tenant laws, including but not limited to Mass. Gen. Laws Ch. 186, ยง 14 and relevant case law interpreting the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

4. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as the subject property is located within the jurisdiction of the Chelsea District Court, where venue is proper.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On or about two months ago, Plaintiff notified Defendant regarding a broken lock on the main entry door of the building at 275 Washington Ave, Chelsea, MA 02150, which failed to latch properly, allowing unauthorized access to the building.

6. Despite Plaintiff's repeated reminders, Defendant failed to remedy the broken lock, thus creating a security risk and breaching the covenant of quiet enjoyment owed to Plaintiff.

7. As a result of the broken lock, unauthorized individuals gained access to the building and stole packages from the hallway, including a package belonging to the Plaintiff valued at approximately $120.

8. Plaintiff discovered evidence indicating that an individual has been sleeping in the basement of the building, further compromising her safety and right to quiet enjoyment of the premises.

9. In response to the security concerns, Plaintiff incurred expenses of approximately $200 to purchase a security camera and a better deadbolt for her own door.

CAUSE OF ACTION

10. Plaintiff alleges a cause of action against the Defendant for breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment, as recognized under Massachusetts law, Mass. Gen. Laws Ch. 186, ยง 14, and relevant case law in Massachusetts including Erickson v. Whistler, 390 Mass. 546 (1983) and Puccio v. Marx, 410 Mass. 438 (1991).

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Jessica Monroe respectfully requests that this Court grant the following relief:

1. Monetary damages in the amount of $320 for losses incurred due to Defendant's breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

2. Any other relief that the Court deems just and proper.
