COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, the Plaintiff, Emily Johnson, by and through undersigned counsel, and for her complaint against the Defendant, Robert Williams, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. The Plaintiff, Emily Johnson, is an individual residing at 567 Elm Street, Somerville, MA 02144.

2. The Defendant, Robert Williams, is the landlord of the property located at 567 Elm Street, Somerville, MA 02144, in which the Plaintiff resides.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. Jurisdiction of this Court is proper pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Ch. 218, ยง 21. Venue is proper in the Somerville District Court as the property at issue is located within its jurisdiction.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

4. The Plaintiff entered into a lease agreement with the Defendant to rent an apartment located at 567 Elm Street, Somerville, MA 02144, commencing on January 1, 2022.

5. The Plaintiff pays the Defendant $1,800 per month in rent.

6. Since approximately six months ago, the occupants of the apartment above the Plaintiff have consistently engaged in disruptive and excessive noise levels, including but not limited to stomping, playing loud music late at night, and moving furniture late into the night.

7. The Plaintiff has made numerous complaints to the Defendant regarding the noise disturbances caused by the upstairs neighbors, and the Defendant responded by stating he "can't control how people live."

8. The Plaintiff's lease with the Defendant entitles her to the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

9. The continuous noise disturbances have severely impacted the Plaintiff's ability to sleep, resulting in an average of four to five hours of sleep per night.

10. The Plaintiff has received a written warning at work for falling asleep at her desk due to lack of sleep.

11. As a result of the noise disturbances, the Plaintiff's doctor prescribed sleep medication costing the Plaintiff $40 per month.

12. The Plaintiff's attempts to mitigate the noise disturbances, including purchasing earplugs, a white noise machine, and rearranging her bedroom, have been unsuccessful.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

COUNT I: BREACH OF COVENANT OF QUIET ENJOYMENT

13. The Plaintiff realleges and incorporates by reference the foregoing paragraphs as if fully set forth herein.

14. Massachusetts recognizes the covenant of quiet enjoyment as a fundamental right afforded to tenants in residential leases, including the Plaintiff's lease with the Defendant.

15. The Defendant's failure to address and remedy the excessive noise disturbances caused by the upstairs neighbors constitutes a breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

16. The Plaintiff has suffered damages as a direct result of the Defendant's breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff respectfully prays for relief as follows:

1. Judgment in favor of the Plaintiff for compensatory damages in the amount of $2,400;
  
2. Prejudgment and postjudgment interest as allowed by law;

3. Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
