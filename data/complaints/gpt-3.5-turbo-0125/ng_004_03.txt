COMPLAINT FOR NEGLIGENCE

COMES NOW, the Plaintiff, Jordan Thompson, by and through undersigned counsel, and for his Complaint against the Defendant, Emily Carter, states as follows:

PARTIES

1. The Plaintiff, Jordan Thompson, is an individual residing at [Your Address], Worcester, Massachusetts.

2. The Defendant, Emily Carter, is the owner of the property located at 45 Maple Street, Worcester, Massachusetts.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

3. On February 10, 2023, at 45 Maple Street, Worcester, Massachusetts, the Defendant, Emily Carter, failed to properly maintain the sidewalk outside the property following a snowstorm.

4. The Defendant only shoveled a narrow path down the middle of the sidewalk, without applying salt or sand to prevent the formation of ice.

5. As a result, the path created by the Defendant turned into a sheet of ice due to melting and refreezing, creating a hazardous condition on the sidewalk.

6. On the mentioned date, the Plaintiff, while delivering groceries to a tenant of the property, slipped and fell on the ice patch, injuring his lower back.

7. The Defendant was informed of the icy condition by the tenants and was notified with photographs, yet failed to take appropriate action to address the dangerous situation.

8. The Plaintiff sustained injuries to his lower back, incurring approximately $2,500 in medical expenses, and lost approximately $600 in wages due to the injury.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

COUNT I - NEGLIGENCE

9. The Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain the sidewalk in a safe condition for pedestrians under Massachusetts law.

10. The Defendant breached this duty by failing to properly clear and treat the sidewalk, allowing a hazardous ice patch to form.

11. The Defendant's breach of duty was the direct and proximate cause of the Plaintiff's injuries and resulting damages.

12. As a result of the Defendant's negligence, the Plaintiff suffered physical injuries, incurred medical expenses, and lost wages.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff, Jordan Thompson, respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in his favor and against the Defendant, Emily Carter, for the following relief:

a. Compensatory damages in the amount of $3,100;
b. Prejudgment interest as allowed by law;
c. Costs and attorney's fees incurred in prosecuting this action; and
d. Any other relief that the Court deems just and proper.
