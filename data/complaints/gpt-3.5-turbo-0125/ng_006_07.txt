COMPLAINT

Now comes the Plaintiff, Alex Johnson, by and through undersigned counsel, and for his Complaint against Defendant Michael Thompson, states as follows:

2. The Defendant, Michael Thompson, is a resident of Massachusetts.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

4. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 223, ยง 9.

5. Venue is proper in Suffolk County Superior Court as the actions giving rise to this Complaint occurred within this county.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

6. On August 15, 2023, while the Plaintiff, Alex Johnson, was biking to work in a marked bike lane on Boylston St in Boston, Massachusetts, the Defendant, Michael Thompson, a rideshare driver, negligently opened his car door without checking for oncoming traffic, causing the Plaintiff to crash into the door and suffer injuries.

7. The Plaintiff was following all traffic rules and wearing proper safety gear when the Defendant's negligent act caused the Plaintiff to sustain road rash on their arms and legs, a chipped front tooth, and damage to their bike frame.

8. The Defendant admitted at the scene of the accident that he was distracted by his phone and did not see the Plaintiff before opening the door.

9. The Defendant subsequently made false statements to his insurance company alleging that the Plaintiff was "weaving in and out of traffic," which is untrue and defamatory.

CAUSES OF ACTION

10. The Defendant owed a duty of care to the Plaintiff as a fellow road user to operate his vehicle in a safe manner and avoid causing harm to others.

11. The Defendant breached this duty by negligently opening his car door without ensuring it was safe to do so, directly causing the Plaintiff's injuries.

12. The Defendant's false statements to his insurance company regarding the Plaintiff's actions constitute defamation.

13. The Defendant's conduct constitutes negligence under Massachusetts common law and statutes, including MGL c. 90, ยง 14.
