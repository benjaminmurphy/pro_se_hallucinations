COMES NOW, Plaintiff Jason Thompson, by and through undersigned counsel, and complains against Defendant HomeGoods Discount Store as follows:

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as the amount in controversy does not exceed the court's jurisdictional limits.

4. Venue is proper in Lowell District Court as the cause of action arose in Middlesex County where the Defendant's place of business is located.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On September 15, 2023, at the premises of HomeGoods Discount Store located at 45 Market Street, Lowell, MA 01852, Plaintiff Jason Thompson was shopping for kitchen wares.

6. Defendant HomeGoods Discount Store had negligently stacked heavy boxes of appliances unsafely on shelves, particularly at heights of five to six boxes without proper securing measures such as straps or barriers.

7. Another customer caused a minor impact to a lower shelf by pushing a cart down the aisle, which resulted in a large toaster-oven box falling from the top shelf, striking Plaintiff on the shoulder and side of the head. As a result, Plaintiff suffered a mild concussion and shoulder strain.

8. Despite the incident report being filled out by the store manager, Defendant HomeGoods Discount Store failed to provide a safe environment for customers by not properly securing and stabilizing the stacked boxes of appliances.

CAUSES OF ACTION

9. The Plaintiff suffered injuries and damages as a direct result of the Defendant's negligence in maintaining a safe shopping environment.

10. Defendant HomeGoods Discount Store breached its duty of care owed to the Plaintiff by negligently stacking and failing to secure the heavy boxes of appliances, causing harm to the Plaintiff.

11. The Defendant's negligence was the direct and proximate cause of the Plaintiff's mild concussion and shoulder strain.

STATUTORY AND CASE LAW

12. Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 231, Section 85 establishes principles of contributory negligence and comparative negligence in negligence cases.

13. In Mehanna v. Shaw's Supermarkets, Inc., 2008 Mass. Super. LEXIS 333 (Mass. Super. Ct. 2008), the court held that a business has a duty to provide a safe shopping environment for customers.

14. In Payton v. Wal-Mart Stores, Inc., 2000 Mass. Super. LEXIS 88 (Mass. Super. Ct. 2000), the court found that a business can be held liable for injuries caused by improperly stacked merchandise.
