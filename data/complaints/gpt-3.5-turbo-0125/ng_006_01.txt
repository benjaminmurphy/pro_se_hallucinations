[Factual Allegations]
4. On September 10, 2023, at approximately [Time], Plaintiff Jason Miller was lawfully riding his bicycle in a marked bike lane on Commonwealth Avenue, Boston, Massachusetts. 5. The sudden and negligent action of the rideshare driver caused Plaintiff to collide with the open door, resulting in injuries to Plaintiff, including road rash on his arms and legs, a chipped front tooth, and damage to his bike frame.
6. Despite the rideshare driver's admission at the scene that he did not see Plaintiff because he was checking his phone, Defendant later submitted false information to their insurance company, falsely alleging that Plaintiff was "weaving in and out of traffic." These false statements have caused harm to Plaintiff's claim for compensation.

[Causes of Action - Negligence]
7. Defendant had a duty to operate its vehicle safely and to check for oncoming traffic before opening the vehicle door.
8. By negligently opening the vehicle door without proper regard for oncoming cyclists, Defendant breached its duty of care to Plaintiff.
9. As a direct result of Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff suffered injuries, medical expenses of approximately $1,500, and lost wages in the amount of $600.
10. Defendant's actions constitute negligence under Massachusetts law.
