**FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS**  
6. On March 15, 2023, at the shared driveway of the duplex at 34 Maple Street in Springfield, Massachusetts, Plaintiff was backing her car into the driveway.  
7. Defendant had previously been informed by Plaintiff of the dangerous placement of an unsecured tall aluminum extension ladder against the wooden fence adjacent to the parking area, where Plaintiff parked her car.  
8. Despite acknowledging the potential hazard and expressing intent to secure or relocate the ladder, Defendant negligently failed to take necessary precautions.  
9. Subsequently, due to a gust of wind, the unsecured ladder fell onto Plaintiff's vehicle, causing damage to the car's hood and windshield.  
10. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant's negligence, Plaintiff incurred expenses related to repair services, including a new windshield and bodywork, requiring the payment of a deductible and the rental of a car while her vehicle was being repaired.

**CAUSES OF ACTION**  
11. The Defendant owed a duty of care to the Plaintiff to secure and maintain property in a safe manner within the shared driveway.  
12. The Defendant breached this duty by negligently failing to secure the ladder, despite knowledge of the potential harm it posed to Plaintiff's vehicle.  
13. The Defendant's negligence was the direct and proximate cause of the damages suffered by the Plaintiff.
