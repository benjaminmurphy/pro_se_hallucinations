FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On or about September 15, 2023, at approximately [time], a fire broke out in a first-floor apartment at 123 Maple Street due to a kitchen-related incident.

6. As per the duty of care owed by the landlord of the property, Defendant Lynn Apartments, LLC was responsible for maintaining functional smoke detectors in the common hallways of the building.

7. The smoke detector located outside Plaintiff's unit had been chirping due to a low battery for several months prior to the incident. Despite multiple notifications by Plaintiff to the landlord, Defendant failed to replace the battery.

8. In the absence of a working smoke detector, Plaintiff was not alerted to the fire until she smelled smoke, by which time the hallway was already filled with thick smoke.

9. As a result, Plaintiff suffered smoke inhalation, leading to her hospitalization for medical treatment. Plaintiff also experienced breathing difficulties as a consequence of the incident.

10. Additionally, Plaintiff's personal belongings were damaged by smoke and water as a result of the fire and subsequent firefighting efforts.

CAUSES OF ACTION

11. Defendant Lynn Apartments, LLC's negligence constitutes a breach of duty owed to Plaintiff under Massachusetts negligence law.

12. Defendant's failure to maintain a working smoke detector directly led to Plaintiff's injuries and damages.
