**COMPLAINT FOR MODIFICATION OF CUSTODY**

---

Now comes the Plaintiff, Jennifer Smith, and for her Complaint for Modification of Custody against the Defendant, Michael Smith, states as follows:

**I. Parties**

1. The Plaintiff, Jennifer Smith, resides at [Your Address], [City], [State], [ZIP Code].

2. The Defendant, Michael Smith, resides at [Defendant's Address], [City], [State], [ZIP Code].

**II. Factual Allegations**

3. On May 15, 2017, the Middlesex Probate and Family Court entered an initial custody order granting primary residence of the child, Emma Smith, to the Defendant, Michael Smith, and limited weekend visitation to the Plaintiff.

4. Since the issuance of the initial custody order, there has been a material change in circumstances. The Plaintiff has completed her education, secured stable employment with regular hours, and established a suitable living environment for the child.

5. The child, Emma Smith, who is now ten years old, has expressed a desire for increased time with the Plaintiff and has indicated this preference to her therapist and through a letter addressed to the Court.

6. The Plaintiff and the child have developed a close and loving relationship, and the child experiences emotional distress at the prospect of being separated from the Plaintiff after short visitations as outlined in the current custody arrangement.

7. Despite the child's expressed desires and the changes in the Plaintiff's circumstances, the Defendant has refused to engage in discussions regarding modification of the custody arrangement and becomes hostile when the child raises the issue.

**III. Requested Modification**

8. The Plaintiff respectfully requests that the Court modify the existing custody order to grant her additional parenting time with the child, Emma Smith, in order to better serve the child's best interests and promote her emotional well-being.

**IV. Prayer for Relief**

WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff prays that this Honorable Court grant the following relief:

1. Modify the existing custody order to provide the Plaintiff with an increased parenting time schedule with the child, Emma Smith.
2. Grant such other and further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.

**CITATION OF AUTHORITY**

1. **Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 208, Section 31:** Provides for the modification of custody orders upon a showing of a substantial change in circumstances.
2. **Yannas v. Frondistou-Yannas, 395 Mass. 704 (1985):** Establishes the standard for modification of custody orders.
3. **Custody of Vaillancourt, 54 Mass. App. Ct. 383 (2002):** Affirms the principle that the child's best interests are paramount in custody modification cases.
4. **Custody of Kali, 439 Mass. 834 (2003):** Emphasizes the importance of the child's wishes in custody modification proceedings.
5. **Adoption of Talik, 84 Mass. App. Ct. 644 (2014):** Discusses the factors considered in modifying custody arrangements.
6. **Custody of Carney, 65 Mass. App. Ct. 316 (2006):** Considers the impact of a parent's circumstances on the child's well-being in custody modifications.
7. **Custody of Derrick, 52 Mass. App. Ct. 280 (2001):** Addresses the importance of maintaining parent-child relationships in custody modifications.
8. **Goodridge v. Department of Public Health, 440 Mass. 309 (2003):** Recognizes the evolving nature of family relationships in custody matters.
9. **Custody of Zia, 85 Mass. App. Ct. 549 (2014):** Discusses the criteria for modifying a custody order to promote the child's best interests.
10. **Custody of Chanel, 457 Mass. 120 (2010):** Considers the impact of parental conflict on custody modifications.
