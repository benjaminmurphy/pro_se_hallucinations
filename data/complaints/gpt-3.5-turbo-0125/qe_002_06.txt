FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On or around November 1, 2025, the Plaintiff noticed water seeping into her basement unit at 123 Maple Street whenever there was heavy rain.

6. The Plaintiff promptly informed the Defendant of the water issue and requested repairs. The Defendant acknowledged the complaint but failed to take necessary action to remedy the situation.

7. Subsequently, a city inspection conducted on an unspecified date confirmed the water issue and discovered additional code violations in the rental property.

8. Due to the persistent water intrusion, the Plaintiff's personal property, including a rug and a bookshelf, sustained damages estimated at $400.

9. As a result of the water problem, the Plaintiff was forced to run a dehumidifier continuously, leading to increased electricity costs.

10. The Defendant's refusal to address the water issue in a timely and effective manner has substantially interfered with the Plaintiff's peaceful enjoyment of the rental premises.

CAUSE OF ACTION

11. The Defendant's failure to adequately respond to and rectify the water issue in the rental property constitutes a breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment, a cause of action recognized under MGL c. 186, ยง14 and supported by Massachusetts case law.
