FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

6. On September 15, 2023, at 100 Maple Street, Lynn, MA 01905, Plaintiff was residing in an apartment managed by the Defendant.

7. The Defendant, as the landlord, had a duty to maintain working smoke detectors in the common hallways of the apartment building.

8. For several months prior to the incident, the smoke detector in the common hallway outside Plaintiff's unit emitted a chirping noise due to a low battery. Despite Plaintiff's repeated notifications, the Defendant failed to replace the battery in a timely manner.

9. On the night of September 15, 2023, a fire broke out in a first-floor apartment, causing smoke to fill the common hallway. Due to the non-functional smoke detector, Plaintiff did not receive a timely warning and was only alerted to the fire by the smell of smoke.

10. As a result, Plaintiff suffered smoke inhalation, experienced difficulty breathing, and required hospitalization for medical treatment. Additionally, Plaintiff's personal belongings were damaged by smoke and water.

11. Plaintiff incurred approximately $4,500 in medical expenses and lost $1,200 in wages due to the incident.

CAUSES OF ACTION

12. Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain functional smoke detectors in the common hallways of the apartment building to protect the residents from foreseeable harm.

13. The Defendant breached its duty of care by failing to promptly address the non-functional smoke detector despite being informed of the issue multiple times.

14. The Defendant's negligence in maintaining the smoke detector directly caused the injuries and damages suffered by Plaintiff.
