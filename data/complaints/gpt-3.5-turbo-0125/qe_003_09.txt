COMPLAINT

Now comes the Plaintiff, Laura Simmons, and for her Complaint against the Defendant David Thompson, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. The Plaintiff, Laura Simmons, is an individual residing at 25 Green Street, Jamaica Plain, MA 02130.

2. The Defendant, David Thompson, is the landlord of the property located at 25 Green Street, Jamaica Plain, MA 02130.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This action arises under Massachusetts landlord-tenant law and specifically alleges a breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

4. This Court has jurisdiction over the subject matter of this action pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 185, Section 1.

5. Venue is proper in the Boston Housing Court, Suffolk County, as the subject property is located within its jurisdiction.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

6. On or around February 1, 2022, the Plaintiff entered into a lease agreement with the Defendant to rent a studio apartment located at 25 Green Street, Jamaica Plain, MA 02130 for a monthly rent of $1500.

7. During the showing of the apartment, the Defendant assured the Plaintiff that prior water stains on the ceiling were from an old leak that had been fully repaired.

8. Shortly after moving in, the Plaintiff experienced water dripping through the ceiling during rainy weather, consistent with the location of the prior water stains.

9. Despite the Plaintiff's complaints, the Defendant has failed to address the ongoing water intrusion issue, dismissing it as a minor cosmetic problem.

10. The water intrusion has escalated, causing damage to the Plaintiff's personal property, including soaking her couch and damaging a laptop, requiring approximately $900 in replacement costs.

11. In addition, the water intrusion has resulted in a musty odor and potential mold growth, causing health concerns for the Plaintiff and limiting her ability to fully utilize the apartment space.

CAUSE OF ACTION

12. The Defendant's failure to effectively address and rectify the ongoing water intrusion issue constitutes a breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment implied in the lease agreement, as provided under Massachusetts law.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court:

a. Enter judgment in favor of the Plaintiff and against the Defendant for damages in the amount of $900, representing the cost of the damaged laptop and personal property.

b. Order the Defendant to undertake immediate and necessary repairs to eliminate the water intrusion issue and remediate any mold growth within the premises.

c. Grant such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
