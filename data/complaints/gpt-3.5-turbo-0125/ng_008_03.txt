COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, the Plaintiff, Jessica Harper, by and through undersigned counsel, and for her Complaint against the Defendant, Quincy Dine-In, alleges as follows:

I. PARTIES

1. Plaintiff, Jessica Harper, is an adult individual residing at [Your Address] in Quincy, Massachusetts.

2. Defendant, Quincy Dine-In, is a business entity registered in Massachusetts, located at 245 Washington Street, Quincy, MA 02169.

II. JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This action arises under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts for damages stemming from the Defendant's negligent actions or omissions.

4. The Superior Court of Barnstable County has jurisdiction over the parties and subject matter of this action. The incident giving rise to this lawsuit occurred at the Defendant's premises located at 245 Washington Street, Quincy, MA 02169.

III. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On July 15, 2023, at Quincy Dine-In, the Defendant's employee dropped a tray of water glasses in the hallway between the kitchen and the dining room. The employee picked up the broken glass but failed to clean up the spilled water and ice, neglecting to place a warning sign.

6. Approximately ten minutes later, Plaintiff decided to use the restroom and unknowingly walked into the wet hallway. The floor was still slick with water, causing Plaintiff to slip and severely twist her knee.

7. Subsequent to the fall, Plaintiff sought medical attention at urgent care, where she was diagnosed with a sprained knee that necessitated a brace and physical therapy for several weeks.

8. It was later revealed that the Defendant's staff members were aware of the hazardous conditions in the hallway, acknowledging they had been "dodging that puddle" prior to Plaintiff's accident.

IV. CAUSES OF ACTION

9. The Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain a safe environment for its patrons, including Plaintiff, at its premises.

10. The Defendant breached its duty by failing to promptly clean up the spilled water and ice, neglecting to warn of the existing hazard, despite actual knowledge of the dangerous condition.

11. The Defendant's negligence directly caused Plaintiff's injuries, resulting in medical expenses of approximately $2,500 and lost wages amounting to $1,200.

V. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Jessica Harper respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in her favor and against the Defendant, Quincy Dine-In, for the following relief:

a. Compensatory damages in an amount to be proven at trial but not less than $3,700;
b. Prejudgment interest;
c. Costs and expenses of this action; and
d. Any further relief as this Court deems just and equitable.
