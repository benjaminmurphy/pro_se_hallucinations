FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. Plaintiff rented the apartment at 24 Chelsea St, Chelsea, MA 02150, from Defendant on June 1, 2022, for a monthly rent of $1500.

6. On or about [date], Plaintiff notified Defendant of a broken lock on the main entry door to the building, which compromises the security of the premises. Despite assured repairs, Defendant failed to address the issue, allowing unauthorized access to the building.

7. Subsequently, unauthorized individuals gained access to the building, resulting in the theft of packages belonging to Plaintiff and other tenants. Plaintiff suffered a loss of approximately $120 due to the stolen package.

8. Plaintiff also discovered evidence of unauthorized individuals sleeping in the building's basement, which has caused Plaintiff significant distress and concerns for their safety.

9. In response to Defendant's failure to address the security issues, Plaintiff purchased additional security measures, including a security camera and a better deadbolt, incurring costs of approximately $200.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

10. Defendant's failure to maintain the security of the premises and address the broken lock on the main entry door constitutes a breach of the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment under Massachusetts law.
