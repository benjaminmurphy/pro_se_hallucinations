JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as the amount in controversy exceeds the minimum jurisdictional limits of this Court.

4. Venue is proper in Hampden County Superior Court as the incident and the parties' residences are within the jurisdiction of this Court.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On March 15, 2023, at 123 Elm St, Springfield, Massachusetts, Plaintiff's car was parked in the shared driveway between her duplex and Defendant's property.

6. Defendant had a tall aluminum extension ladder leaning against a wooden fence adjacent to the parking area where Plaintiff parked her car.

7. Despite Plaintiff's repeated concerns about the safety of the ladder, Defendant failed to secure or move the ladder away from the parking area.

8. On a windy day, a gust of wind caused the ladder to fall over and strike Plaintiff's car, resulting in substantial damage to the car hood and windshield.

9. As a direct result of the ladder falling and striking her car, Plaintiff incurred expenses for the repair of the damages, including the cost of a new windshield and bodywork.

CAUSES OF ACTION

10. Defendant owed a duty of care to Plaintiff to ensure that his property did not pose an unreasonable risk of harm to others, including securing the ladder to prevent foreseeable harm.

11. Defendant breached this duty of care by failing to secure the ladder despite Plaintiff's warnings about the potential danger it posed.

12. Defendant's breach of duty was the proximate cause of the damages sustained by Plaintiff's car, constituting negligence under Massachusetts law.
