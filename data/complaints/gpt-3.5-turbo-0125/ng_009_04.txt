JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 218, Section 21, as the amount in controversy does not exceed the statutory limit for district court jurisdiction.

4. Venue is proper in this district where the apartment building, the alleged negligence, and the resulting injuries occurred.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On September 12, 2023, at 150 Maple Street, Lynn, MA 01905, a fire broke out in a first-floor apartment of the building owned by the Defendant.

6. Plaintiff resided on the third floor of the same building where the fire occurred.

7. Prior to the fire, the smoke detector located in the common hallway outside Plaintiff's unit had been chirping due to a low battery.

8. Despite notifying the Defendant multiple times about the malfunctioning smoke detector, the Defendant failed to take prompt and adequate action to replace the battery.

9. As a result of the non-functioning smoke detector, Plaintiff was not alerted to the fire until she smelled smoke in the hallway, when it was already filled with thick smoke.

10. Plaintiff suffered smoke inhalation and difficulty breathing while trying to evacuate the building, resulting in her hospitalization.

11. Additionally, Plaintiff's personal belongings were damaged by smoke and water during the fire.

CAUSES OF ACTION

12. The Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain a safe premises for its tenants, including ensuring that smoke detectors were in proper working order in common areas.

13. The Defendant breached its duty of care by failing to promptly address the malfunctioning smoke detector despite notice from the Plaintiff.

14. The Defendant's negligence in failing to maintain the smoke detector directly caused Plaintiff's injuries and damages.
