COMPLAINT FOR NEGLIGENCE

COMES NOW, the Plaintiff Jessica Miller, by and through undersigned counsel, and for her Complaint against the Defendant Emily Johnson, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. The Plaintiff, Jessica Miller, is an individual residing at [Your Address], in the City of Worcester, County of Worcester, Massachusetts.

2. The Defendant, Emily Johnson, is an individual who owns and is responsible for the maintenance of the property located at 123 Maple Street, Worcester, MA 01603.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as the incident giving rise to this action occurred within Worcester County, Massachusetts.

4. Venue is proper in the Worcester District Court as the Defendant's property, where the incident occurred, is located within the jurisdiction of this Court.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On February 10, 2023, at the property located at 123 Maple Street, Worcester, MA 01603, owned and maintained by the Defendant, Emily Johnson, Plaintiff Jessica Miller was delivering groceries to a tenant of the property.

6. Despite being responsible for the maintenance of the sidewalk abutting her property, the Defendant failed to adequately clear the sidewalk of snow and ice following a snowstorm.

7. The Defendant shoveled a narrow path down the middle of the sidewalk but failed to put down any salt or sand to prevent the path from turning into a dangerous sheet of ice due to melting and refreezing.

8. Upon walking up the sidewalk to deliver groceries, the Plaintiff encountered the ice patch on the sidewalk, causing her to slip and fall backward, resulting in a sprained lower back injury.

9. The Defendant was informed by tenants residing at the property that the sidewalk was icy, and was even sent pictures of the hazardous conditions. Despite this notice, the Defendant negligently failed to take corrective action and dismissed the concerns raised by the tenants.

CAUSES OF ACTION

10. The Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain the sidewalk in a reasonably safe condition for individuals lawfully using the property, including the Plaintiff.

11. The Defendant breached this duty of care by failing to properly clear the sidewalk of snow and ice, creating a hazardous condition that led to the Plaintiff's injury.

12. The Defendant's negligent actions directly caused the Plaintiff's injuries and resulting damages.

STATUTORY AND CASE LAW VIOLATIONS

13. The Defendant's actions constitute negligence under Massachusetts General Laws, including but not limited to:
