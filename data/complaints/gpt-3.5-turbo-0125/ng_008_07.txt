FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On May 15, 2023, at Quincy Casual Dining, LLC, a server dropped a tray with several water glasses in the hallway between the kitchen and the dining room.

6. Despite quickly clearing the broken glass, the server failed to promptly clean up the water and ice spill, leaving the tile floor wet and hazardous.

7. The server neglected to place any warning signs or barriers to alert patrons of the dangerous condition.

8. Approximately ten minutes later, Plaintiff Jessica Hughes slipped on the wet floor while walking to the bathroom, injuring her knee in the fall.

9. Subsequent to the incident, it came to light that the restaurant staff was aware of the hazard, with employees admitting to "dodging that puddle" prior to Plaintiff's fall.

10. As a result of the slip and fall, Plaintiff sustained a sprained knee necessitating a brace and physical therapy.

CAUSES OF ACTION

11. Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain its premises in a reasonably safe condition for the benefit of its patrons, including Plaintiff.

12. Defendant breached its duty of care by negligently failing to promptly clean up the spill, failing to warn of the dangerous condition, and failing to take precautions to prevent injuries.

13. Defendant's negligence directly caused Plaintiff's injuries and resulting damages.
