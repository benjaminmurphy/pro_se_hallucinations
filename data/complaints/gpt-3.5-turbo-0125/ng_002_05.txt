3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 218, Section 21. Venue is proper in the District Court of Newton as the events giving rise to this action occurred in Chestnut Hill Park, Newton, MA.

III. FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

4. On September 15, 2023, at Chestnut Hill Park in Newton, MA, Plaintiff was jogging on a designated path in the park.

5. Despite posted signs requiring all dogs to be on leashes, Defendant's large dog was running loose in the park.

6. Defendant was present approximately thirty feet away from Plaintiff, talking with friends while holding the leash over his shoulder but not attached to the dog.

7. The dog suddenly charged at Plaintiff, barking loudly, causing Plaintiff to fear for her safety.

8. In an attempt to evade the charging dog, Plaintiff swerved off the path and tripped over a tree root, falling down a small embankment and breaking her wrist.

9. Multiple witnesses observed the incident, confirming the sequence of events.

10. Plaintiff incurred medical expenses of approximately $5,000 for treatment of her broken wrist and suffered wage loss in the amount of $1,200 due to the injury.

IV. CAUSE OF ACTION: NEGLIGENCE

11. Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain control over his dog in compliance with the park rules requiring all dogs to be leashed.

12. Defendant breached this duty by allowing his dog to run loose, endangering park visitors like Plaintiff.

13. Defendant's breach of duty was the direct and proximate cause of Plaintiff's injuries, as she fell while attempting to avoid the dog.

14. The negligence of Defendant resulted in damages to Plaintiff, including medical expenses and lost wages.

V. PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in her favor and against Defendant as follows:

a. Compensatory damages in the amount of $6,200 for medical expenses and lost wages;

b. Any further relief that the Court deems just and equitable.
