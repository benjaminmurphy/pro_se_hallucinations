COMPLAINT  

COMES NOW, the Plaintiff, Emily Carter, by and through undersigned counsel, and for her Complaint against the Defendant, James Thompson, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff, Emily Carter, is an individual residing at 45 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301.

2. Defendant, James Thompson, is the landlord of the residential property located at 45 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as it concerns a landlord-tenant dispute within the County of Brockton, Massachusetts.

4. Venue is proper in this district as the subject property is located within the territorial jurisdiction of this Court.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On May 1, 2022, the Plaintiff entered into a rental agreement with the Defendant for the premises located at 45 Maple Street, Brockton, MA 02301.

6. The rental agreement obligates the Defendant to provide the Plaintiff with a habitable dwelling, including the provision of hot water.

7. On November 10, 2025, the Plaintiff discovered that the hot water in her apartment had ceased functioning.

8. The Plaintiff immediately notified the Defendant of the issue and requested repairs.

9. Despite the Plaintiff's repeated complaints and requests for action, the Defendant failed to repair the hot water within a reasonable time.

10. As a result, the Plaintiff has been deprived of hot water for nearly a month, causing her to incur additional expenses for alternative bathing arrangements and discomfort due to the lack of basic amenities.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

11. The Defendant's failure to provide hot water constitutes a breach of the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment, as guaranteed by Massachusetts landlord-tenant laws.

12. Such breach has caused the Plaintiff financial losses, inconvenience, and emotional distress.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court grant the following relief:

a. Judgment in favor of the Plaintiff for compensatory damages in the amount of $450.

b. Any further relief that this Court deems just and proper.

c. Costs and attorneys' fees associated with this action.

DEMAND FOR JURY TRIAL

Plaintiff demands a jury trial on all issues so triable.

This Complaint is based on the Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 186, Section 14, which establishes the implied warranty of habitability and quiet enjoyment for residential tenants. Additionally, relevant caselaw in Massachusetts includes McKenna v. Begin, 363 Mass. 338 (1973), Boston Housing Authority v.
