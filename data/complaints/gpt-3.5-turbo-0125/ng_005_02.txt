COMPLAINT FOR NEGLIGENCE

COMES NOW, the Plaintiff, Jessica Turner, by and through undersigned counsel, and for her complaint against the Defendant, Michael Adams, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff, Jessica Turner, is a resident of Springfield, Massachusetts.

2. Defendant, Michael Adams, is a resident of Springfield, Massachusetts and owns the property located at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, Massachusetts.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over the subject matter of this action pursuant to Mass. Gen. Laws Ann. ch. 218, ยง 3.

4. Venue is proper in Hampden Superior Court, as the incident giving rise to this action occurred at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, Massachusetts.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On March 15, 2023, at the property located at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, Massachusetts, owned by the Defendant, a ladder owned and placed by the Defendant fell due to windy conditions and damaged the Plaintiff's vehicle, specifically the hood and windshield.

6. Prior to this incident, the Plaintiff had informed the Defendant of her concerns regarding the safety of the ladder being placed in close proximity to where she parked her vehicle.

7. The Defendant had a duty to exercise reasonable care in maintaining his property and ensuring that objects on the premises were secured to prevent harm to others.

8. The Defendant breached the duty of care owed to the Plaintiff by failing to secure the ladder or move it to a safe location despite being made aware of the potential danger it posed to the Plaintiff's property.

CAUSES OF ACTION

9. The Defendant's actions constitute negligence under Massachusetts law. The Defendant breached his duty of care owed to the Plaintiff, which directly resulted in damages to the Plaintiff's vehicle.

10. The Defendant's negligence was a proximate cause of the Plaintiff's injuries and damages.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Jessica Turner respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in her favor and against the Defendant Michael Adams, and grant the following relief:

a. Economic damages in the amount of $0 for the cost of repairing the dented hood and cracked windshield of the Plaintiff's vehicle;
b. Costs and expenses incurred as a direct result of the Defendant's negligence;
c. Any further relief that the Court deems just and proper.
