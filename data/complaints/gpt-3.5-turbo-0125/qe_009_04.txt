COMPLAINT

Now comes the Plaintiff, Jessica Greene, and for her Complaint against the Defendant, Mark Thompson, alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. The Plaintiff, Jessica Greene, is an individual residing at 456 Maple Street, Springfield, MA.

2. The Defendant, Mark Thompson, is the landlord and owner of the property located at 456 Maple Street, Springfield, MA.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as the amount in controversy does not exceed the jurisdictional limit of this court.

4. Venue is proper in this district as the property subject to this dispute is located within this district.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. Plaintiff entered into a residential lease agreement with Defendant for the rental of an apartment located at 456 Maple Street, Springfield, MA, commencing on January 15, 2022.

6. Defendant, as landlord, failed to provide and maintain electricity in one of the bedrooms of the apartment, rendering the room uninhabitable for approximately six weeks.

7. Despite Plaintiff's complaints and requests for repairs, Defendant failed to promptly address the electricity issue in the bedroom, citing complications with the building's old wiring.

8. As a result of the lack of electricity in the bedroom, Plaintiff has been unable to use the room as a home office, incurring additional expenses on a daily basis at coffee shops for workspace and food, totaling around $15 per day, and at a coworking space at a monthly cost of $150.

9. Plaintiff has been forced to cram all office belongings into the living room, disrupting her living conditions and causing inconvenience and distress.

CLAIM FOR RELIEF

10. Plaintiff alleges that Defendant's failure to provide and maintain electricity in the bedroom constitutes a breach of the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment under Massachusetts law.

PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Jessica Greene respectfully requests that this Court enter judgment in her favor and grant the following relief:

a. Damages in the amount of $720, representing additional expenses incurred due to the uninhabitable bedroom;
b. Any other relief the Court deems just and proper.
