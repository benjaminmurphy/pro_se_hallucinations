**Jurisdiction and Venue**

4. The amount in dispute exceeds the jurisdictional threshold of this court. Therefore, this court has subject matter jurisdiction over this action.

5. Venue is proper in the Hampden Superior Court as the incident leading to this action occurred at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, MA.

**Factual Allegations**

6. On March 15, 2023, at 123 Maple Street, Springfield, MA, Plaintiff was driving her car into the shared driveway when a ladder owned by the Defendant, Mark Dorsey, fell due to windy conditions.

7. Despite Plaintiff's prior warnings to Defendant about the potential danger of the ladder falling onto her property, Defendant failed to secure the ladder properly, leading to the ladder falling onto the hood and windshield of Plaintiff's car.

8. As a result of the ladder falling, Plaintiff's car sustained damage to the hood and a cracked windshield, necessitating repairs and replacement.

**Causes of Action**

9. Defendant's actions constitute negligence under Massachusetts law. The Defendant owed a duty of care to maintain his property in a safe manner and failed to do so, directly causing harm to the Plaintiff.

10. The Defendant's negligence in failing to secure the ladder led to the damage sustained by Plaintiff's car.

**Prayer for Relief**

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Jessica Turner respectfully requests the following relief from this Honorable Court:

1. Compensation for the property damage caused to Plaintiff's car due to Defendant's negligence.
2. Reimbursement for any out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Plaintiff as a result of the incident.
3. Any further relief that this Honorable Court deems just and proper.

Plaintiff demands a trial by jury on all issues so triable.
