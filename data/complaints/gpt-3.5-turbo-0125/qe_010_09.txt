COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, Plaintiff, Jessica Tran, by and through undersigned counsel (if any), and for her Complaint against Defendant, Linda Harrison, alleges as follows:

1. Parties:
a. Plaintiff, Jessica Tran, is an individual residing at 123 Maple Street, Lowell, MA 01851.
b. Defendant, Linda Harrison, is the landlord of the aforementioned property located at 123 Maple Street, Lowell, MA 01851.

2. Jurisdiction and Venue:
a. This Court has jurisdiction over this matter as the subject property is located within its jurisdiction.
b. Venue is proper in the District Court of Lowell, Massachusetts, as the property in question is situated within the jurisdiction of this Court.

3. Facts:
a. Plaintiff began renting the property located at 123 Maple Street, Lowell, MA 01851 from Defendant on June 1, 2020.
b. Plaintiff pays monthly rent of $1200 to Defendant.
c. Plaintiff discovered the presence of mold in the bathroom of the rented property, specifically in the corner of the shower, which has since spread to the ceiling.
d. Plaintiff reported the mold issue to Defendant, who inspected the bathroom and attributed the problem to Plaintiff.
e. Plaintiff was blamed by Defendant for not running the exhaust fan enough and for taking hot and prolonged showers.
f. Although Plaintiff does use the exhaust fan while showering and enjoys hot showers, Defendant refuses to address the inadequate ventilation in the bathroom.
g. Plaintiff has developed respiratory issues, including coughing, congestion, and wheezing, since the appearance of mold, which Plaintiff's doctor attributes to the mold.
h. Plaintiff had to discard a shower curtain and towels due to mold damage, incurring approximately $60 in expenses.
i. Despite Defendant's acknowledgment of the existing mold, Defendant has not taken adequate steps to remedy the situation.

4. Causes of Action:
COUNT I: Breach of the Covenant of Quiet Enjoyment
a. Defendant breached the implied covenant of quiet enjoyment by failing to provide habitable living conditions, specifically by neglecting to address the mold issue in the bathroom, resulting in health hazards and interference with Plaintiff's use and enjoyment of the premises.
b. Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 186, Section 14, guarantees tenants the right to quiet enjoyment, which includes the right to a healthy living environment free from mold and other potential health hazards.
c. The case law in Massachusetts, such as Boston Housing Authority v. Hemingway, 363 Mass. 184 (1973) and McKenna v. Begin, 29 Mass. App. Ct. 914 (1990), confirms the landlord's duty to maintain premises in a habitable condition under the covenant of quiet enjoyment.

5. Prayer for Relief:
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Jessica Tran respectfully requests that this Court:
a. Find Defendant in breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment.
b. Order Defendant to remediate the mold issue in the bathroom within a reasonable time frame.
c. Award Plaintiff damages in the amount of $500, for costs incurred due to the mold damage.
d. Grant any further relief that the Court deems just and proper.

6. Jury Demand:
Plaintiff demands a trial by jury on all triable issues.
