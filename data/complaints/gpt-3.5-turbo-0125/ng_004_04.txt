COMPLAINT

COMES NOW, Plaintiff Jessica Turner ("Plaintiff"), by and through undersigned counsel, and for her complaint against Defendant Anna Brooks ("Defendant"), alleges as follows:

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff, Jessica Turner, is an individual residing at [Your Address], [City], [State], [Zip Code].

2. Defendant, Anna Brooks, is the owner of the premises located at 123 Maple Street, Worcester, MA 01603, and is believed to be a resident of Worcester, Massachusetts.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE

3. This action arises out of the negligence of the Defendant in maintaining the premises at the above-mentioned address. The jurisdiction of this Court is proper pursuant to Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 223, Section 9.

4. Venue is proper in Worcester Superior Court as the incident giving rise to this action occurred at 123 Maple Street, Worcester, MA 01603.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On February 15, 2023, at approximately [Time], Plaintiff was delivering groceries to a tenant at 123 Maple Street, Worcester, MA 01603.

6. Despite being aware of the accumulation of ice on the sidewalk outside the premises, Defendant negligently failed to remove the ice, sand, or salt the area following a snowstorm that occurred on [Date of Snowstorm].

7. The path shoveled by Defendant subsequently turned into a sheet of ice due to melting during the day and refreezing at night.

8. As Plaintiff walked up the sidewalk in the course of her delivery, she slipped on the icy surface and fell backward, sustaining a lower back injury requiring medical treatment.

9. The tenants of the property had notified Defendant of the hazardous condition by sending her pictures of the ice, to which Defendant responded that "the sun will take care of it."

10. As a direct result of the fall, Plaintiff incurred medical expenses of approximately $2,500 and lost approximately $1,000 in wages after missing a week of work.

CAUSE OF ACTION

11. Defendant's negligence in failing to maintain the premises in a safe condition caused Plaintiff's injuries, constituting a breach of the duty of care owed to individuals lawfully on the premises.

12. Defendant's failure to adequately address the known hazardous condition of the sidewalk constitutes negligence under Massachusetts common law.

c) Any other relief that this Court deems just and proper.
