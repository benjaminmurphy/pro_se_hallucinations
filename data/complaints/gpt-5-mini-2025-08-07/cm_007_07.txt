COMPLAINT FOR MODIFICATION OF CUSTODY ORDER

1. Parties and Residence
   1.1 Plaintiff: Laura Chester, pro se. Telephone: (617) 555-0123; email: psm1443@gmail.com.
   1.2 Defendant: Michael Chester.
   1.3 Minor child: Samantha Chester, age 12 (born 2013). Both parents are the legal parents of Samantha.
   1.4 Venue and jurisdiction are proper in the Bristol County Probate and Family Court because the parties and the child have been subject to court orders entered in this county and the child resides in Massachusetts.

2. Prior Order
   2.1 On April 15, 2018, the Probate and Family Court entered a custody/parenting time order providing for equal physical custody of the parties’ child, Samantha, on a one-week-on, one-week-off alternating schedule (the “2018 Order”).
   2.2 That order has remained in effect since its entry and currently governs the parties’ custody and parenting time arrangements.

3. Basis for Modification — Material Change in Circumstances
   3.1 The Plaintiff alleges that, since entry of the 2018 Order, there has been a substantial and material change in circumstances that affects the welfare and safety of the minor child and warrants modification of the existing custody arrangement.
   3.2 Specifically, the Defendant has been diagnosed with a progressive neurological condition (described to the Plaintiff by the Defendant and by the Defendant’s treating physician). Since the diagnosis the Defendant’s mobility and balance have declined; the Defendant has sustained multiple falls at home.
   3.3 The Defendant’s treating physician has advised the Defendant that, because of the Defendant’s diagnosis and impaired mobility and balance, the Defendant should not be left alone with a child in situations where stairs, transferring (e.g., from wheelchair to bed), or other situations requiring a prompt physical response may be present.
   3.4 Notwithstanding this medical advice and the changed circumstances, the Defendant insists on maintaining the same alternating-week, equal-time schedule established in the 2018 Order and has continued to accept unsupervised custody of the child during his weeks without arranging for appropriate adult assistance or supervision.
   3.5 On multiple occasions during the Defendant’s custodial time the Defendant has fallen, and the child has been asked to assist the Defendant with transfers (moving the Defendant from wheelchair to bed) and to retrieve items for the Defendant. The child has had to physically assist the Defendant on at least one occasion.
   3.6 The Plaintiff alleges that leaving the child alone with the Defendant under these conditions poses a risk of physical harm to the child and also places inappropriate adult caregiving responsibilities on a 12-year-old.
   3.7 The foregoing facts constitute a material change in circumstances since entry of the 2018 Order: the Defendant’s progressive medical condition and attendant safety risks were not present, or were not of the same severity, at the time the 2018 Order was entered.

4. Best Interests of the Child
   4.1 The Plaintiff asserts that modification of the 2018 Order is necessary to protect Samantha’s safety, welfare, and best interests.
   4.2 The Plaintiff alleges that an arrangement providing primary residential custody to the Plaintiff, with parenting time for the Defendant tailored to his current medical needs and limited so that Samantha is not put in the position of assisting the Defendant with transfers or acting as caregiver, is in Samantha’s best interests.
   4.3 The Plaintiff further alleges that temporary modification of the current schedule pending a full hearing (including, if appropriate, an evidentiary hearing and medical evaluations) is necessary to protect the child from immediate risk.

5. Relief Requested
   WHEREFORE the Plaintiff requests that the Court:
   5.1 Find that there has been a material and substantial change in circumstances since entry of the 2018 Order that justifies modification of the existing custody and parenting time order.
   5.2 Modify the 2018 Order to provide that Plaintiff shall have primary physical custody (primary residence) of Samantha, subject to a parenting time schedule for the Defendant that ensures the child’s safety and does not require or allow the child to perform caregiving tasks for the Defendant.
        - Proposed interim schedule (requested temporarily and pending a full hearing): The child’s primary residence shall be with Plaintiff; Defendant shall have supervised or supported parenting time as follows:
            a. Daytime parenting time on alternating weekends (Friday after school or 3:00 p.m. through Sunday at 7:00 p.m.), provided that Defendant has a competent adult caregiver (age 18 or older) physically present in the home during all times he has the child; OR
            b. If Defendant is able to demonstrate (by medical evidence or by proof of a primary caregiver/attendant present during visits) that he can safely care for the child without placing duties on the child, the Court may grant unsupervised daytime parenting time consistent with safety conditions set by the Court; AND
            c. Exchanges to occur at a neutral public location or other location as the Court directs; no exchanges shall take place on stairs or in situations identified by the Defendant’s physician as unsafe.
        - The Plaintiff requests that the Court order that Defendant shall not: require the child to assist with transfers or other adult care tasks; leave the child unsupervised in proximity to stairs or other hazardous conditions; or engage in caregiving activities that place the child at risk.
   5.3 Order that Defendant shall provide Plaintiff and the Court with documentation of the Defendant’s current treating physician’s assessment of the Defendant’s physical abilities and the recommended safe accommodations and limitations concerning caregiving for the child; and that the Defendant shall produce any relevant medical records or assessments relating to his neurological condition.
   5.4 Order that the parties and the child attend, as the Court directs, any evaluations or reunification/safety-oriented parenting programs, home assessments, or parenting capacity evaluations deemed necessary by the Court, including, if appropriate, a gifted or specialized medical evaluation to assess Defendant’s ability to provide safe unsupervised parenting time.
   5.5 Order any temporary relief necessary to protect the child pending a final hearing, including temporary modification of physical custody and supervision or conditions on parenting time.
   5.6 Award the Plaintiff such further relief as the Court deems just and proper to protect Samantha’s safety and best interests, including but not limited to costs and such other orders as to the Court seem fair and equitable.

6. Legal Standard (general)
   6.1 The Plaintiff will rely on the statutory and judicial standards governing modification of custody orders under Massachusetts law, including (a) demonstration of a material and substantial change in circumstances since the prior order; and (b) a determination that modification of custody is in the child’s best interests. The Plaintiff will present factual evidence, including medical records and witness testimony, to support the requested modification.

7. Prayer for Relief
   Wherefore, Plaintiff requests that this Court:
   a. Set this matter down for hearing;
   b. Grant interim relief modifying physical custody as requested herein pending a full hearing;
   c. After hearing, enter an order modifying the 2018 Order to grant primary physical custody to Plaintiff and to provide a parenting time schedule for Defendant consistent with the child’s safety and best interests; and
   d. Grant such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.

Plaintiff requests a trial by the Court on all contested issues.
