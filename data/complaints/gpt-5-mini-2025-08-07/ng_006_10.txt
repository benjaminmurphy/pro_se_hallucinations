COMPLAINT — NEGLIGENCE

NOW COMES the Plaintiff, Mark Johnson, pro se, and for his Complaint against Defendant Ryan Smith alleges as follows:

PARTIES, JURISDICTION, AND VENUE

1. Plaintiff Mark Johnson is an individual and a resident of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. Plaintiff’s telephone number is (617) 555-0123 and his email address is psm1443@gmail.com.

2. Defendant Ryan Smith is an individual believed to reside in Massachusetts. Plaintiff will supplement Defendant’s address if required.

3. This action arises out of a motor-vehicle/bicycling collision that occurred on September 12, 2023 at or near 55 Commonwealth Ave, Boston, Massachusetts, which is located in Suffolk County.

4. Jurisdiction and venue are proper in the Suffolk County Superior Court pursuant to the general venue and subject matter jurisdiction of this Court because the events giving rise to Plaintiff’s claims occurred in Suffolk County and the amount in controversy is within the jurisdictional limits of this Court.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On September 12, 2023, at approximately [time not recorded], Plaintiff was lawfully operating his bicycle and traveling to work in Boston, Massachusetts, while riding in a marked bicycle lane on 55 Commonwealth Ave. Plaintiff was wearing a helmet, obeyed all applicable traffic signs and signals, and was operating in a safe and prudent manner.

6. At the same time and place, Defendant was operating (or in control of) a motor vehicle that was stopped and parked in a curbside loading zone immediately adjacent to the marked bicycle lane.

7. As Plaintiff approached the Defendant’s vehicle, Defendant suddenly opened the driver’s door into the bicycle lane without checking mirrors, shoulder-checking, yielding, or otherwise ensuring that it was safe to open the door.

8. Plaintiff had no time to stop, swerve, or otherwise avoid the open door. He collided with the edge of the door, was thrown from his bicycle, and struck the pavement.

9. At the scene of the crash, Defendant admitted to Plaintiff that he “didn’t see” Plaintiff because he was checking his phone at or immediately before opening the vehicle door. Defendant’s admission was made contemporaneously and under circumstances indicating its reliability.

10. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant’s actions and omissions, Plaintiff suffered physical injuries, property damage, and economic losses, including but not necessarily limited to:
   a. Road rash to Plaintiff’s arms and legs;
   b. A chipped front tooth requiring dental care;
   c. A bent bicycle frame requiring replacement of the bicycle;
   d. Medical and dental expenses of approximately $1,500 to date;
   e. Lost wages in the approximate amount of $500; and
   f. Pain, suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life in amounts to be proven at trial.

11. After the incident, Defendant (or Defendant’s insurer) made an alternative, inconsistent statement that Plaintiff was “weaving in and out of traffic.” That allegation is false. Plaintiff had been riding in a marked bicycle lane and exercising lawful and reasonable care for his own safety at all relevant times.

CAUSE OF ACTION
Count I — Negligence (Against Defendant Ryan Smith)

12. Plaintiff repeats and realleges paragraphs 1 through 11 as if fully set forth herein.

13. At all relevant times, Defendant owed Plaintiff, as other road users including bicyclists, a duty to exercise reasonable care under the circumstances to avoid causing unreasonable risk of harm. This duty included the obligation to check for approaching traffic (including bicyclists) before opening a vehicle door into a bicycle lane or otherwise entering or leaving a vehicle adjacent to a traffic lane, to refrain from engaging in unsafe conduct while occupying a motor vehicle (including using a cell phone while performing tasks requiring lookout), and to otherwise operate and use his vehicle in a manner safe for others.

14. Defendant breached his duty of care by, among other things:
   a. opening his vehicle door into the bicycle lane without ensuring it was safe to do so;
   b. failing to check mirrors or look for approaching cyclists before opening the door;
   c. opening the door while distracted by his phone; and
   d. otherwise failing to exercise the degree of care that a reasonably prudent person would have exercised under similar circumstances.

15. Defendant’s breaches of duty were the proximate and direct cause of the collision and of Plaintiff’s injuries, losses, and damages described above.

16. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant’s negligence, Plaintiff suffered and will continue to suffer physical pain, disability, permanent scarring (if applicable), emotional distress, medical and dental expenses, lost earnings, property damage, and other damages in amounts to be proven at trial.
