FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

5. On or about February 10, 2023, following a snowstorm, Defendant or Defendant’s agents/representatives undertook to clear a narrow path down the middle of the sidewalk in front of the Property at 123 Maple Street, Worcester, MA, but did not spread salt, sand, grit, or any de-icing material on the cleared path.

6. Over the following days, the narrow cleared path melted during daytime temperatures and refroze at night, creating a continuous sheet of ice on the middle of the sidewalk in front of the Property.

7. On February 10, 2023, Plaintiff was lawfully on the sidewalk at 123 Maple Street delivering groceries to a tenant of the Property. The sidewalk was icy due to the conditions described above.

8. While walking on the sidewalk, Plaintiff’s feet slipped on the ice, Plaintiff fell backward and landed heavily on her lower back.

9. The tenants of the Property informed Plaintiff after the fall that they had previously texted Defendant and sent Defendant photographs of the icy sidewalk and that Defendant had responded that “the sun will take care of it.”

10. Defendant knew or reasonably should have known of the dangerous icy condition on the sidewalk (including by direct notice from tenants and by the obvious condition of the sidewalk) and had the ability and responsibility to address the hazard (including by spreading sand, salt, grit, or otherwise making the sidewalk safe).

11. As a direct and proximate result of the fall caused by the icy hazardous condition on the sidewalk, Plaintiff suffered a sprained lower back and related injuries.

12. Plaintiff sought medical treatment for the injuries she sustained as a result of the fall. Plaintiff’s reasonable and necessary medical expenses are approximately $1,200.

13. As a direct and proximate result of the injuries, Plaintiff missed approximately one week of work, for which she suffered lost wages of approximately $800.

14. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant’s negligence, Plaintiff has suffered pain, discomfort, inconvenience, diminished enjoyment of life, and other damages in amounts to be proven at trial.

CAUSE OF ACTION
Count I — Negligence

15. Plaintiff repeats and realleges paragraphs 1 through 14 as if fully set forth herein.

16. At all times relevant hereto, Defendant owed a duty to individuals lawfully on or using the sidewalk adjacent to the Property to exercise reasonable care to keep the sidewalk in a reasonably safe condition, to warn of hazardous conditions known to her, and to take reasonable steps to remedy dangerous conditions of which she knew or should have known.

17. Defendant breached that duty by, among other things:
    a. Clearing only a narrow path down the center of the sidewalk but failing to apply sand, salt, grit, or other de-icing materials to prevent the formation of ice;
    b. Failing to take reasonable steps to remedy the icy condition after being put on notice by tenants and after the hazardous condition was present and observable; and
    c. Failing to warn lawful users of the sidewalk of the hazardous icy condition.

18. Defendant’s acts and omissions were negligent, careless, and willful, and constituted a breach of the duties owed to Plaintiff.

19. As a direct and proximate result of Defendant’s negligence, Plaintiff sustained physical injuries (including a sprained lower back), incurred medical expenses (approximately $1,200), lost wages (approximately $800), and suffered pain and emotional distress and other damages for which Defendant is liable.
